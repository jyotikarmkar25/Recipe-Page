Kheer

Ingrediants

  • Rice
  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Flavorings & Aromatics(cardamom,safron,essence)
  • For Garnish(Almonds,Pistachios,Cashew,Raisins)
  • Optional(Ghee,Makhana,Rose Petals)

Description

Kheer is a traditional, creamy South Asian rice pudding made by slow-cooking milk, sugar, and rice (or alternatives like vermicelli/tapioca) until thick. Often flavored with cardamom, saffron, and nuts, this popular dessert is a staple at festivals, weddings, and celebrations, served either hot or chilled.

Recipe

  • Prep Rice: Rinse and soak cup Basmati rice for 20-30 minutes.
  • Boil Milk: Bring litre full-fat milk to a boil in a heavy-bottomed pan.
  • Cook Rice: Add soaked rice to the milk and simmer on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice is fully cooked and soft.
  • Thicken: Continue to cook until the mixture reaches a creamy, thick consistency.
  • Sweeten & Flavor: Add tablespoons of sugar (or to taste), cardamom powder, and saffron.
  • Add Nuts: Stir in chopped almonds, cashews, or pistachios (optionally, fry them in ghee first).
  • Serve: Serve hot or chilled.
