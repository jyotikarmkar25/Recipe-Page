Dal Chawal

Dal(Lentils)

  • Lentils(Moong Dal)
  • Aromatics(Onion,Ginger,Garlic,Tomatoes,Green Chili)
  • Spices(Turmeric Powder,Red Chili Powder,Garam Masala, Salt)
  • Tempering(Tadka)
  • Garnish(Coriander leaves,Lemon Juice)

Chawal

  • Rice
  • Water
  • Ghee Or Oil

Description

Dal Chawal is an Indian dish which is healthy for us because it contains a lot of nutrients, it is low in fat and high in fibers, it also connects us with our culture.

Recipe

Dal

  • Rinse & Soak: Thoroughly wash 1 cup of toor dal (pigeon peas) or mixed dal (moong/masoor) and soak it in water for 20–30 minutes.
  • Pressure Cook: Combine soaked dal with 2.5–3 cups of water, ½ tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp salt, and a few drops of oil in a pressure cooker.
  • Cook: Cook for 3–4 whistles until the dal is soft and mushy.
  • Temper (Tadka): In a separate pan, heat 2 tbsp ghee or oil. Add 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 tsp mustard seeds, and a pinch of asafoetida (hing).
  • Aromatics: Add 4–5 minced garlic cloves, 1 chopped onion, 1-2 chopped green chilies, and a few curry leaves. Sauté until golden brown.
  • Final Touch: Add 1 finely chopped tomato (optional) and ¼ tsp red chili powder. Cook until the tomatoes are soft, then pour this tempering into the cooked dal.
  • Simmer: Add salt to taste, bring to a boil, and simmer for 5 minutes. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Rice

  • Rinse: Wash 1 cup of Basmati or regular long-grain rice 3-4 times until the water runs clear to remove excess starch.
  • Soak: Soak the rice for 15-20 minutes.
  • Cook: Add 2 cups of water and 1 tsp salt to the rice. Cook in a pressure cooker (1-2 whistles) or a boiling pot until fluffy.
  • Add Ghee: Add 1 tsp ghee before serving for extra flavor and aroma.

Serving

  • Serve hot steaming rice with a generous amount of dal on top.
  • Pair with roasted papad, pickle, or onion salad.
