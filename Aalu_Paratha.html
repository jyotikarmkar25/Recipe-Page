Aalu Ke Parathe

Ingrediants

  • Potatoes
  • Wheat Flour
  • Green Chilies
  • Coriander Leafs
  • Ghee/Oil/Butter
  • Salt

Aloo paratha is a popular North Indian breakfast flatbread made from whole wheat dough stuffed with a savory, spiced mixture of mashed potatoes, green chilies, onions, and coriander. These unleavened breads are rolled out and shallow-fried with ghee or oil on a hot griddle (tawa) until golden brown and crispy.

Recipe

  • Knead: Mix flour, salt, oil, and water to make a soft dough; rest for 15-20 minutes.
  • Stuffing: Mash potatoes fine (no lumps) and mix with all spices, herbs, and salt; divide into balls.
    • Roll: Stuff the dough ball, seal tightly, and roll gently into a 6-7 inch circle.
  • Cook: Cook on a hot tawa, applying ghee/oil until golden brown spots appear on both sides.
  • Serve: Pair with butter, yogurt, or pickle.
